# AI Slop & Fluff Avoidance Guide

This document is the definitive reference for purging AI-generated language patterns from all content — essays, social posts, infographics, and any written material published under Vedang Vatsa's name.

## Why This Matters

AI-generated text has recognizable fingerprints. Readers — especially tech-savvy ones — can detect it instantly. Using AI slop:
- Destroys authenticity and trust
- Makes content indistinguishable from every other AI-assisted account
- Signals laziness, not thought leadership
- Undermines the credibility of someone with 22 real publications

## Tier 1: Absolute Banned Words & Phrases

These words are NEVER acceptable. If they appear, rewrite the sentence entirely.

| Banned | Why It's Slop | What To Write Instead |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| explore / explore into | #1 AI tell | "examine," "look at," or just state the topic directly |
| landscape | Vague filler | "market," "field," "industry," or name the specific thing |
| tapestry | Nobody says this | Remove. Describe what you actually mean |
| | Throat-clearing | Delete it and state the thing |
| in the realm of | Pompous filler | "in" |
| it's notable that | Throat-clearing | Delete. Just state the thing |
| it's important to note | Same as above | Delete |
| in today's world | Generic opener | Name the specific context |
| in today's rapidly evolving | Double generic | Delete entirely |
| major shift / important | Hyperbole | Describe the specific change |
| model shift | Overused academic | Describe what actually shifted |
| at the end of the day | Cliché filler | Delete or rewrite with actual conclusion |
| food for thought | Weak ending | End with a specific question or implication |
| only time will tell | Lazy non-conclusion | State what you actually think will happen |
| the question remains | Avoids taking a position | Answer the question or state your view |
| a double-edged sword | Cliché | Describe the two specific sides |

## Tier 3: AI Sentence Patterns

These aren't individual words but recognizable AI writing structures.

### ❌ The Hedged Non-Statement
```
"While there are certainly challenges, the potential benefits could 
potentially outweigh the drawbacks in certain scenarios."
```
**Fix**: Pick a side. "The benefits outweigh the costs because [specific reason]."

### ❌ The Triple Adjective Stack
```
"This new, groundbreaking, and major technology..."
```
**Fix**: One precise adjective or none. "This technology does [specific thing]."

### ❌ The Meaningless Framework
```
"When we consider the intersection of AI and governance, 
several key factors emerge..."
```
**Fix**: State the factors. "AI governance fails at three points: [A], [B], and [C]."

### ❌ The Circular Definition
```
"Artificial intelligence is a form of intelligence that is artificial 
in nature, created by humans to simulate human-like intelligence."
```
**Fix**: Give a concrete example instead of defining.

### ❌ The False Balance
```
"On one hand, AI could help humanity. On the other hand, it could 
pose risks. The truth likely lies somewhere in between."
```
**Fix**: Take a position. "AI will [do X] because [evidence]. The risk is [specific risk]."

### ❌ The Grandiose Opener
```
"In an era of unprecedented technological advancement, the world 
stands at a crossroads..."
```
**Fix**: Start with a specific fact, number, or observation. "DJI holds 70% of the drone market. Five years ago it was 40%."

### ❌ The Vague Conclusion
```
"As we move forward, it will be interesting to see how these 
developments unfold in the coming years."
```
**Fix**: Make a prediction or ask a specific question. "If this trend continues, by 2028 [specific outcome]."

## Detection Checklist

Run every piece of content through these checks before publishing:

### Quick Scan (30 seconds)
- [ ] Search for "explore," "landscape," "tapestry," "" — delete all
- [ ] Check first sentence — does it start with "In today's..." or "In an era..."? Rewrite
- [ ] Check last sentence — does it say "only time will tell" or "remains to be seen"? Rewrite
- [ ] Count adjectives in first paragraph — more than 2 per sentence = rewrite

### Deep Scan (2 minutes)
- [ ] Read every sentence aloud. Does any sentence sound like a press release? Rewrite
- [ ] Is there a specific number, name, or example in every paragraph? If not, add one
- [ ] Does the piece take a clear position? If you can't summarize the stance in one sentence, it's too hedged
- [ ] Remove every sentence that starts with "It is" or "There are" — rewrite in active voice
- [ ] Check for the word "just" — it's usually filler, delete it

### Social Post Scan (15 seconds)
- [ ] No hashtags beyond 2
- [ ] No "I'm excited/thrilled/humbled"
- [ ] No "major shift/model shift"
- [ ] No engagement bait questions
- [ ] Under 200 words for short-form platforms

## Replacement Principles

When you remove AI slop, follow these rules:

1. **Specific beats general**: "revenue grew 40%" beats "significant growth"
2. **Short beats long**: "China leads in drones" beats "China has established a dominant position in the global drone manufacturing landscape"
3. **Active beats passive**: "AI replaces jobs" beats "Jobs are being replaced by AI"
4. **Concrete beats abstract**: "Google fired 12,000 people" beats "Major tech companies have undergone workforce reductions"
5. **Position beats balance**: "This will fail because X" beats "There are both pros and cons"
6. **Question beats declaration**: "What happens when every company has an AI agent?" beats "The rise of AI agents will transform business"

```bash

```

Zero results = clean. Any matches must be fixed before publishing.
