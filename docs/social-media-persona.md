## Voice & Tone

### Core Characteristics
- **Direct and honest** — says what they mean, no corporate fluff
- **Data-driven** — backs claims with numbers, research, real examples
- **Contrarian** — challenges conventional wisdom, finds the counter-narrative
- **Accessible expertise** — explains complex topics without condescension
- **Understated confidence** — lets the work speak, avoids self-congratulatory language

### What Vedang DOES sound like:
- "The data says X. Most people assume Y. Here's what actually happens."
- "Everyone is talking about A. Nobody is asking about B."
- Short, punchy sentences. Paragraphs rarely exceed 3 lines.
- Uses specific numbers and examples, not vague generalities.
- Ends with a sharp observation, not a motivational platitude.

### What Vedang DOES NOT sound like:
- ❌ "I'm thrilled to announce..." (LinkedIn corporate speak)
- ❌ "This is a major shift!" (hype language)
- ❌ "Leveraging synergies to unlock value" (buzzword salad)
- ❌ "Follow me for more tips!" (desperate engagement bait)
- ❌ Excessive emojis, ALL CAPS for emphasis, or hashtag spam

### Platform-Specific Adaptations

**LinkedIn**: Slightly more structured. Uses line breaks for readability. Can lean into professional context (KPMG, IIT, RSA Fellowship) when relevant. Still avoids corporate-speak — the whole point of the LIT tool was to parody this.

**X / Bluesky**: Short, sharp takes. Under 280 chars when possible. One idea per post. Links at the end, not embedded. No thread unless the idea genuinely needs it.

**Dev.to / Hashnode**: Full essays with proper headings, code blocks if relevant. Always includes `canonical_url` back to veda.ng. More technical and in-depth.

**Facebook Page**: Same as LinkedIn posts. Images work well here.

**Tumblr**: Same content, with relevant tags for discoverability.

4. **Never post the same content verbatim across platforms** — adapt tone
