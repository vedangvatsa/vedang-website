This module is entirely hands-on. By the end, you will have installed a wallet, received free testnet cryptocurrency, and executed your first on-chain transaction. No real money required - we start on a test network so you can learn the mechanics safely before touching real assets.

    <AccordionItem value="item-1">
        <AccordionTrigger className="text-lg">Step 1: Install MetaMask (10 minutes)</AccordionTrigger>
        <AccordionContent className="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-none text-base">
            A digital wallet is your primary tool for interacting with Web3. It stores your crypto, acts as your login for dApps, and signs transactions. We'll use MetaMask - the most widely used wallet with over 30 million monthly users.

            **Installation:**

            1. Open your browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Brave) and go to <Link href="https://metamask.io/download/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">metamask.io/download</Link>.
            2. Click "Install MetaMask for [your browser]." It installs as a browser extension.
            3. Click "Create a new wallet." Agree to the terms.
            4. Create a strong password. This is for your local device only - it is NOT your seed phrase.
            5. **The Seed Phrase - READ THIS CAREFULLY:** MetaMask will show you a 12-word "Secret Recovery Phrase." This is the master key to your wallet.
                * **Write it down on paper.** Not in a text file, not in a screenshot, not in your notes app. Paper.
                * **Store the paper somewhere safe.** A locked drawer, a safe, or a safety deposit box.
                * **Never share it with anyone.** No legitimate service, person, or website will ever ask for your seed phrase. If someone asks for it, they are trying to steal your assets.
                * **If you lose your seed phrase, you lose access to your wallet permanently.** There is no "forgot password" button. There is no customer support to call. This is the trade-off of self-custody.
            6. Confirm the seed phrase by selecting the words in the correct order.
            7. Done. You now have a Web3 wallet. Your wallet address (starting with `0x`) is your public identity on the blockchain.

            **Self-Custodial vs. Custodial:** MetaMask is a *self-custodial* wallet - you hold the keys, you control the assets. A *custodial* wallet (like a Coinbase account) means the company holds your keys for you. For learning and true Web3 participation, self-custodial is the way.

    <AccordionItem value="item-2">
        <AccordionTrigger className="text-lg">Step 2: Get Free Testnet ETH (5 minutes)</AccordionTrigger>
        <AccordionContent className="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-none text-base">
            Before spending real money, you should practice on a **test network** (testnet). Testnets are exact copies of the Ethereum blockchain, but the ETH on them has no real value. You can get it for free from a "faucet."

            **Getting Sepolia Testnet ETH:**

            1. **Switch MetaMask to the Sepolia network:** Click the network dropdown at the top of MetaMask (it says "Ethereum Mainnet" by default). Click "Show test networks" in settings if you don't see them. Select **"Sepolia test network."**
            2. **Copy your wallet address:** Click your account name in MetaMask to copy your `0x...` address to your clipboard.
            3. **Visit a faucet:** Go to <Link href="https://cloud.google.com/application/web3/faucet/ethereum/sepolia" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Google Cloud Web3 Faucet</Link> or <Link href="https://www.alchemy.com/faucets/ethereum-sepolia" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Alchemy Sepolia Faucet</Link>.
            4. **Paste your address** into the faucet and click "Send." You'll receive 0.05-0.5 Sepolia ETH within a few seconds.
            5. **Check MetaMask:** You should now see a balance in your MetaMask wallet on the Sepolia network. This ETH is free and has no real value - it's purely for learning.

            **Why testnet first?** Every mistake on a testnet is free. Wrong address? No loss. Approved a bad contract? No loss. Once you're confident with the mechanics, you can switch to mainnet with real assets.
        </AccordionContent>

    <AccordionItem value="item-3">
        <AccordionTrigger className="text-lg">Step 3: Your First On-Chain Transaction (10 minutes)</AccordionTrigger>
        <AccordionContent className="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-none text-base">
            Now let's do something real - swap one token for another on a decentralized exchange. This is the most fundamental Web3 action: a trustless, peer-to-peer trade with no intermediary.

            **Making Your First Swap on Uniswap (Testnet):**

            1. Go to <Link href="https://app.uniswap.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">app.uniswap.org</Link>.
            2. Click **"Connect Wallet"** in the top right. Select MetaMask. A popup will appear - click "Connect." This is how you "log in" to Web3: no email, no password, just your wallet.
            3. Make sure you're on the **Sepolia network** (Uniswap should detect it automatically from MetaMask).
            4. In the swap interface, set the "From" token to **ETH** and the "To" token to any available test token (like UNI).
            5. Enter a small amount (like 0.01 ETH).
            6. Click **"Swap."** MetaMask will pop up and ask you to confirm the transaction. You'll see:
                * **Gas fee:** The small fee paid to the network validators to process your transaction. On testnet, this costs nothing real.
                * **Transaction details:** What you're swapping, how much, and the estimated output.
            7. Click **"Confirm"** in MetaMask.
            8. Wait 15-30 seconds. Your transaction is now being processed by the Sepolia network.
            9. Done! You've just executed a decentralized trade. No bank, no broker, no middleman.

            **What just happened, technically:**
            * Your wallet signed a transaction with your private key (proving you authorized it).
            * The transaction was broadcast to the Sepolia network.
            * Validators included your transaction in a block.
            * Uniswap's smart contract executed the swap according to its code.
            * The new tokens appeared in your wallet.

            This entire process - from signing to settlement - happened in under a minute, without any human intermediary approving or processing it.

        <AccordionTrigger className="text-lg">Step 4: Explore the Ecosystem</AccordionTrigger>
        <AccordionContent className="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-none text-base">
            Now that you've completed your first transaction, here are three things to try next:

            * **Look up your transaction on Etherscan:** Go to <Link href="https://sepolia.etherscan.io/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">sepolia.etherscan.io</Link> and paste your wallet address. You'll see your transaction history - every action on the blockchain is public and verifiable. This is what "transparency" means in practice.
            * **Get a Web3 username:** Go to the <Link href="https://app.ens.domains/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Ethereum Name Service (ENS)</Link> and search for a `.eth` name. On mainnet, you can register one as your human-readable wallet address (e.g., `vedang.eth` instead of `0x7a3b...`).
            * **Browse dApps:** Visit <Link href="https://dappradar.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">DappRadar</Link> to see the most popular decentralized applications across DeFi, gaming, and social. Each one connects to your wallet the same way Uniswap did.

            **When you're ready for real assets:** Switch MetaMask back to "Ethereum Mainnet." Buy a small amount of ETH through a regulated exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, or Gemini), withdraw it to your MetaMask wallet address, and repeat the same steps. The interface is identical - the only difference is that the assets are real.

            **Start small.** Only use money you can afford to lose entirely. The goal is to learn the mechanics, not to invest.

    <AccordionItem value="item-5">
        <AccordionTrigger className="text-lg">Staying Safe: Scams and Red Flags</AccordionTrigger>
        <AccordionContent className="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-none text-base">
            <SecurityRedFlags />

            Web3 gives you more control, but that also means more responsibility. Scams are common, especially targeting beginners. Knowing the patterns is your best defense.

            * **Phishing:** Fake websites or social media accounts that look like real projects. They ask you to "connect your wallet" or "enter your seed phrase." A legitimate project will never ask for your seed phrase. If a site asks for it, close the tab immediately.
            * **Rug Pulls:** A team creates a token or NFT project, hypes it up to attract investment, and then disappears with the money. Red flags include anonymous teams, unrealistic promises of returns, and no working product.
            * **Malicious Approvals:** Some dApps ask you to "approve" unlimited access to your tokens. This gives the smart contract permission to move your tokens at any time. Only approve the exact amount you need, and revoke old approvals regularly using <Link href="https://revoke.cash/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">revoke.cash</Link>.
            * **Too-Good-to-Be-True Offers:** Messages promising free tokens, guaranteed returns, or "airdrop" rewards that require you to connect your wallet to an unknown site. These usually drain your wallet once you approve a transaction.

            **Rules to live by:**

            1. Never share your seed phrase. Ever. With anyone.
            2. Always double-check URLs before connecting your wallet. Bookmark the sites you use regularly.
            3. Use a separate "burner" wallet with small amounts for trying new or unverified projects.
            4. If something sounds too good to be true, it almost certainly is.
            5. Research the team, the code, and the community before putting money in.
