Web3 is not a utopia. It's a young, evolving technology with immense potential but also significant hurdles. This final module explores the open questions and what comes next.

<Accordion type="single" collapsible className="w-full mt-4">

        <AccordionContent className="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-none text-base">
            The potential of Web3 is vast. By building on open, permissionless, and user-owned infrastructure, we can create:
            
            * **A Fairer Creator Economy:** Artists, musicians, and writers can publish their work as NFTs, capturing more of the value and building a direct relationship with their audience, free from platform intermediaries.
            * **User-Owned Platforms:** Social media, gaming, and other platforms can be owned and governed by their users through DAOs, ensuring the rules are fair and the value is distributed among those who create it.
            * **A Global, Open Financial System (DeFi):** Decentralized Finance aims to rebuild the traditional financial system on open rails, providing access to services like lending, borrowing, and trading to anyone with an internet connection, without needing a bank.
            * **Portable Digital Identity:** Your wallet becomes your universal, self-owned identity, letting you move between applications without giving up control of your data.
        </AccordionContent>

    <AccordionItem value="item-2">
        <AccordionTrigger className="text-lg">The Challenges on the Horizon</AccordionTrigger>
        <AccordionContent className="prose dark:prose-invert max-w-none text-base">
            Despite the promise, Web3 faces significant challenges that need to be solved for mass adoption:
            
            * **Scalability:** Decentralized networks are inherently slower and more expensive to use than centralized ones. A transaction on Ethereum can sometimes cost several dollars and take seconds to confirm. Solutions like "Layer 2s" (e.g., Arbitrum, Optimism, Base) are being built to address this by processing transactions off-chain, but the technology is still maturing.
            * **User Experience (UX):** The current UX is too complex for the average user. Managing seed phrases, paying gas fees, and navigating different blockchain networks are major points of friction. Abstracting this complexity away is the single biggest challenge for onboarding the next billion users.
            * **Regulation:** Governments around the world are still grappling with how to regulate cryptocurrencies, DAOs, and DeFi. This legal and regulatory uncertainty creates risk for builders and users alike.
            * **Security and Education:** The self-custody model puts a huge burden on the user. Scams are rampant, and without proper education, new users are highly vulnerable. Building safer wallets and better educational resources is paramount.
